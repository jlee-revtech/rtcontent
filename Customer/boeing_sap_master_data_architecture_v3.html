Every financial posting in S/4HANA writes a single, enriched line item to the Universal Journal (ACDOCA). That line item assembles its 400+ fields from a web of interconnected master data objects. Rather than one tangled diagram, this document traces each relationship chain independently — then shows how they converge into a single ACDOCA posting for core A&D transaction scenarios.

Activity Type Rate = The Indirect Rate Engine

The Activity Type rate ($125/hr) = Cost Center planned costs / planned activity quantity. The fully burdened rate also includes Fringe, OH, and G&A — applied via allocation cycles that cascade through the cost center hierarchy. Each pool absorbs into the next, building the "wrap rate" that DCAA validates against disclosed practices.

CAS 418: Cascading Allocation Structure

Fringe applies to base labor. Overhead applies to labor + fringe. G&A applies to total cost input. This cascading structure — implemented through SAP cost center hierarchies and allocation cycles — must match the contractor's CAS 418 disclosure statement exactly. Deviation = noncompliance. The cost center hierarchy IS the rate pool structure.

Key Architectural Principles
Why This Matters

Single Source of Truth in ACDOCA

No separate FI and CO ledgers. One line item carries GL, cost center, WBS, profit center, segment, material, plant, and personnel simultaneously. Every master data misconfiguration immediately manifests as a financial misstatement — there is no reconciliation buffer between modules.

Cost Accuracy = Master Data Integrity

Wrong personnel cost center = wrong activity rate = wrong actual cost on every WBS that employee charges. At scale — 10,000 engineers, millions of hours — this creates systemic cost reporting failures that DCAA will identify during audit, potentially resulting in questioned costs, billing adjustments, and rate recalculations across every active contract.

Indirect Rate Accuracy is Structural

The entire rate engine (Fringe, OH, G&A) is built on cost center hierarchies and allocation cycles. A hierarchy that drifts from CAS Board disclosures creates DCAA audit exposure across every active contract. This requires continuous governance, not one-time setup.

Segment Reporting Cascades from Profit Center

The ACDOCA Segment field is an attribute of the Profit Center and must align to the organization's financially reported segments in the 10-K/10-Q (e.g., BCA, BDS, BGS at Boeing) and, for defense business units, to CAS segments. One incorrect Profit Center on a $500M WBS misstates revenue between segments. This is a SOX control point requiring automated validation.
