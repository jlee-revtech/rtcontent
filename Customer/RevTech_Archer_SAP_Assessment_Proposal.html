Section 01

Executive Summary

Revelation Technologies proposes two engagement options to address the challenges and opportunities in Archer Aviation's SAP S/4HANA environment.

Archer is live on SAP S/4HANA, but the current implementation was designed with limited scope and does not provide a scalable foundation for producing multiple aircraft. Demand management signals are unreliable, the costing strategy is inconsistent across plants (some plants use standard or moving average valuation, others expense on receipt and track costs at the top line), and the current configuration does not give leadership the accounting, finance, and demand alignment data they need to make decisions at scale.

Archer has also initiated a transition from PEO to Siemens OpCenter as its Manufacturing Execution System (MES), which adds another dimension to any solution design - any changes to the SAP environment need to account for where the MES integration is headed.

This proposal presents two options that can be executed independently or sequentially:

Option 1 - SAP PS / PMMO Proof of Concept ($180,000): A targeted 12-week engagement to assess Archer's current state, build a working proof of concept for SAP Project Systems and PMMO (Grouping, Pegging, and Distribution) in Archer's environment, and deliver a from/to change impact assessment.

Option 2 - AI Data & Process Assessment ($225,000): A broader 12-week assessment across all functional workstreams, evaluating people, process, technology, and data readiness - with a focus on identifying what needs to change at a foundational level before Archer can take advantage of AI and advanced analytics.

RevTech's Recommendation

We recommend Archer execute Option 2 before Option 1.

Project Systems and PMMO are powerful capabilities, but they should not be used as a band-aid for more systemic people, process, data, and technology issues. The current challenges with demand management, inconsistent costing strategies, and limited ERP utilization are symptoms of foundational gaps that need to be understood and addressed first.

Option 2 gives Archer a full picture of the current state across all workstreams and produces a prioritized roadmap that puts PS/PMMO in the right place in the sequence - after the prerequisites are in place, not before. This approach reduces the risk of building a POC on top of an unstable foundation and ensures that when PS/PMMO is implemented, it has the supporting data, processes, and integrations to deliver real value.

That said, both options are structured to stand on their own, and we are prepared to execute either one based on Archer's priorities and timeline.

SAP PS / PMMO Proof of Concept

A targeted engagement to assess Archer's current state, build a working proof of concept for SAP Project Systems with PMMO, and deliver a change impact assessment with implementation recommendations.

What is PMMO?

PMMO (Project Manufacturing Management & Optimization) is SAP's S/4HANA capability for project-based manufacturing. It addresses a core challenge for aircraft manufacturers: how to consolidate procurement and production across multiple aircraft programs for cost efficiency while maintaining per-program cost traceability. PMMO operates through three integrated functions:

FunctionWhat It DoesWhy It Matters
GroupingGroups demand across multiple demand sources (aircraft programs, contracts, projects) into shared procurement and production orders.Fewer POs, better lot sizes, lower unit costs
PeggingAllocates grouped replenishments back to the individual demand sources that generated the original demand.Tracks which program gets which units
DistributionDistributes actual costs from grouped orders back to individual demand sources based on pegging assignments.Accurate per-program cost traceability

For Archer, PMMO with Project Systems would replace the current flat planning approach with a structured model that scales across multiple aircraft builds, gives finance accurate per-program costing, and aligns demand signals across the production network.

MES Integration Considerations

Archer's planned transition from PEO to Siemens OpCenter is factored into this engagement. The PS/PMMO design will account for OpCenter as the target MES, ensuring the project structure and production order model supports the future integration for shop floor confirmations, goods movements, and production order feedback.

Risks & Mitigations

Risk

Inconsistent Costing Foundation

The current plant-level costing inconsistencies (standard/moving average vs. expensed on receipt) may limit the effectiveness of PS/PMMO cost distribution in the POC. Results may not fully represent production-scale behavior until costing is harmonized.

Mitigation

Phase 1 Costing Baseline

Phase 1 documents the costing strategy per plant before the POC begins. The POC design will account for the current state while the Phase 3 deliverables will include costing harmonization recommendations for full implementation.

Risk

Concurrent MES Migration

The PEO-to-OpCenter transition creates a moving target for integration design. PS/PMMO relies on MES for shop floor confirmations and goods movements - if OpCenter timelines shift, the integration assumptions in the POC may need to be revisited.

Mitigation

Dual-State Design

The POC will be designed to work with the current PEO integration while documenting the target-state OpCenter integration model. Phase 3 includes explicit MES transition sequencing recommendations.

Risk

Data Quality & Completeness

The POC requires representative data in the sandbox environment. If master data (material masters, BOMs, cost center structures) is incomplete or inconsistent, POC scenarios may not produce meaningful results.

Mitigation

Early Data Requirements

Data requirements will be defined during kickoff and validated in Phase 1. RevTech does not need production data, but needs data that is representative of real operating conditions. Gaps identified early will be addressed before the POC build begins.

Risk

SME Availability & Timeliness

Delayed access to Archer subject matter experts or slow feedback cycles could compress sprint timelines and reduce the depth of deliverables.

Mitigation

Pre-Engagement Scheduling

SME requirements and interview schedules will be defined during kickoff and tracked weekly. RevTech will provide session agendas and materials in advance. Deliverable review timelines are built into each phase's review week.

Risk

Scope Expansion During Discovery

Discovery may reveal undocumented configurations, workarounds, or process variations that expand the assessment scope beyond what is estimated.

Mitigation

Change Control

Scope is defined by the deliverables listed in this proposal. Discoveries that fall outside the agreed scope will be documented and managed through formal change control. RevTech will flag scope-expanding findings early rather than absorbing them silently.

Risk

Environment Access Delays

The POC requires a sandbox or development environment with representative configuration and data. Delays in provisioning or refreshing the environment will directly impact the Phase 2 timeline.

Mitigation

Environment Readiness as Phase 1 Exit Criteria

Environment provisioning and data loading requirements will be identified in Phase 1 and treated as an exit criterion before Phase 2 begins. This gives Archer four weeks of lead time to prepare the environment.
